Spiked Eggnog Cupcakes

I wanted to make something fun for the holiday party at my office. These cupcakes are so fucking yummy and the boozy frosting is totes TDF. I found that the frosting was v.v. strong right after I made it - but the next day the alochol had some time to wear off a bit and that were perfect. I hope you enjoy them as much as I did!

Ingredients

Cupcake
  • 7 ounces all-purpose flour
  • 2 ounces cornstarch
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 2 1/2 teaspoons fresh grated nutmeg, plus more for topping
  • 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon, plus more for topping
  • 1 cup good quality eggnog
  • 2 tablespoons bourbon
  • 1 tablespoons pure vanilla extract
  • 6 ounces unsalted butter, room temperature
  • 14 ounces granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
Boozy Frosting
  • 2 cups bourbon
  • 3 large egg whites
  • 8 ounces granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1 teaspoon light corn syrup
  • 10 ounces unsalted butter, room temperature and cut into small pieces
  • 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract

Directions

For the cupcakes:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F. Place the oven racks in the upper middle and lower middle positions.

  2. Line your cupcake pans with paper liners.

  3. Whisk all dry ingredients together except for the sugar.

  4. Combine the liquid ingredients in a separate bowl.

  5. In a stand mixer with the paddle attachment, cream the butter and sugar together on low for 3-5 minutes.

  6. Add the eggs one at a time on low and then scrape down the sides of the bowl. Add the dry and wet ingredients, do not overwork the batter.

  7. Scrape down the sides of the bowl and mix for another 20 seconds on medium.

  8. Use a 2 oz scoop to distribute the batter evenly between cupcake liners.

  9. Bake for 22 minutes, or until a knife or toothpick comes out clean.

  10. Cool to room temperature for 25-30 minutes before removing from pan and applying the frosting.

For the boozy buttercream:

  1. In a small saucepan, reduce 2 cups of bourbon on high heat until you have around 3-4 tablespoons. This shit is flammable, so keep an eye on it. If you accidentally flambé the booze, put a lid on the pot for a minute to extinguish the flame. Allow the reduced bourbon to cool to room temperature.

  2. Add sugar, water and corn syrup to a saucepan. Attach a candy thermometer to the side.

  3. Boil and allow sugar mixture to reach the soft ball stage (235-340 degrees F).

  4. While the sugar is coming up to temperature, whip the egg whites in a stand mixer with the whisk attachment. Whip them until they form a soft peak.

  5. With the mixer on medium speed, carefully pour the syrup down the side of the mixing bowl. Turn the speed up to high and whip until the meringue is at a stiff peak and room temperature. You can speed up this process by placing freezer bags filled with ice around the bowl. I saw this on “Martha Bakes” so you know it’s legit.

  6. When the meringue is cool, slowly add the soft butter while mixing on high.

  7. Once all of the butter is incorporated, add the cinnamon, vanilla and bourbon.

  8. I grated a little fresh nutmeg on top right before I served them. It really brought out the eggnog flavors. Then I sprinkled some golden sugar on top.
